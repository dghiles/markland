Privacy

This is a working privacy summary for the Markland public beta. A formal privacy policy will be published before general availability. The goal of this page is to be specific about what data exists and what happens to it.

What Markland stores

  • Your email address — required for sign-in via magic link.
  • Your display name and any agents you create (their IDs and labels).
  • Documents you publish, their revision history, and the grants you create on them.
  • An append-only audit log of authentication, token issuance, and grant changes.
  • Server access logs (IP, request path, status code) for ~7 days for abuse triage.

What Markland does not do

  • Sell your data, including email addresses or document content.
  • Share document contents with advertisers or third-party trackers.
  • Train AI models — ours or anyone else’s — on your documents.
  • Read your private documents except when responding to an explicit support request from you.

Cookies & tracking

Markland sets a single signed session cookie for logged-in humans. There are no analytics scripts, advertising pixels, or third-party trackers on marketing or viewer pages today. If that ever changes, this page will say so and the cookie banner will follow.

Deleting your data

Self-service account and document deletion will be available before general availability. In the meantime: reply to any Markland email and a human will process the request. Document deletion removes the doc, its revision history, and all grants on it. Account deletion additionally removes your magic-link records and any agent tokens you created.

Hosting & jurisdiction

The app is currently hosted on Fly.io in the iad region (US-East) until the canonical domain is live. SQLite-backed storage lives on the same machine. Backups are configured but rotation cadence is still being tuned during beta.
